Mythology

What is Mythology?

Mythology is a collection of legends, myths, and tales from a certain culture. Generally, these myths are deeply intertwined with religion and are used to study the culture of ancient socieities.
While there are tons of different mythos thorughout history, Roman and Greek mythology are most known, followed by Norse Mythology.

Interested in Greek Mythology?

Greek mythology is one of the older mythos in ancient history. Egyptian mythology is older, but the Greek mythos relates to Western society in a much more prominent way.

Interested in Roman Mythology?

Roman mythology is probably the most popular mythos in the modern day. The Romans adapted the Greek mythos and added their rich culture, art, and language, creating a mythos which is beautiful in its descriptions and significant in its revelations.

Interested in Norse Mythology?

Norse mythology, while not as well known, is vastly different from the Roman and Greek mythos. With a more brutal culture and a heavier focus on war, Norse mythos delves into the glories and consequences of battle.
